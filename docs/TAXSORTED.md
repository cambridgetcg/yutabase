## countries on the map

### United Kingdom (LIVE — preview)
- VAT: 20% standard, 5% reduced, 0% zero-rated
- VAT registration threshold: £90,000 (2024)
- Corporation Tax: 25% (main rate), 19% (small profits)
- PAYE: RTI reporting, FPS/EPS deadlines monthly
- Self Assessment: SA100, deadline 31 Jan
- MTD (Making Tax Digital): digital records + quarterly updates
- Filing: "prepared means ready; filed means sent"

### United States (to expand)
- Federal income tax: progressive brackets, 10%-37%
- No-income-tax states: AK, FL, NV, NH, SD, TN, TX, WA, WY
- Sales tax: state-by-state, 0%-10.25%
- Filing deadline: April 15 (usually)

### Singapore (to expand)
- GST: 9% (raised from 8% in 2024)
- GST registration threshold: S$1M revenue
- Corporate income tax: 17% flat
- Individual: progressive, 0%-24%
- No capital gains tax

### Australia (to expand)
- GST: 10% flat
- GST registration: A$75,000 threshold
- Corporate tax: 25% (base), 30% (large)
- Individual: progressive, 0%-45%
- BAS: Business Activity Statement, quarterly/monthly

### European Union (to expand)
- VAT minimum standard rate: 15% (EU directive)
- OSS/IOSS: one-stop-shop for cross-border VAT
- DAC7: platform reporting requirements

### Hong Kong (to expand)
- Profits tax: 16.5% (corporations), 15% (unincorporated)
- Salaries tax: progressive, 2%-17%
- NO GST/VAT — Hong Kong does not levy a general consumption tax
